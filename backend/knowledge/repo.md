# RBI repo rate and EBLR (educational)

## KB:repo-001

The **repo rate** is the rate at which the RBI lends overnight to commercial banks against eligible securities. It is the **policy rate** and the headline number that moves the entire short end of Indian rates. A repo cut signals an easier monetary stance; a repo hike signals a tightening stance. RBI's Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) meets **once every 2 months** to vote on it.

## KB:repo-002

**EBLR (External Benchmark Lending Rate)** — since October 2019, all new floating-rate retail and MSME loans must be linked to an external benchmark. Most banks use the **repo rate** itself; a few use 91-day or 182-day T-bill yields. Your loan rate is then `EBLR + spread`, where the spread is fixed at sanction and reflects the bank's cost-of-funds, operating cost, credit-risk premium, and margin.

## KB:repo-003

**Reset cycle** — repo-linked loans must reset at least every 3 months. In practice, when the RBI cuts the repo, your home loan EMI (or tenure) drops within ~one quarter; when it hikes, the opposite happens. The mechanism is much faster than the older MCLR / BPLR regimes, where transmission could take 6–12 months.

## KB:repo-004

**Tenure adjustment vs EMI adjustment** when the rate moves:

* Default behaviour at almost every Indian retail bank: **keep EMI constant, change tenure**. A 25-bp hike on a 20-year loan can extend the tenure by ~6–10 months without you noticing in your bank statement.
* You can request the bank to switch to **EMI adjustment** instead — pay more (or less) every month and keep the tenure fixed. This is usually a free request in writing.

For long-tenure home loans, EMI adjustment is more transparent — you see the rate change in your monthly outflow rather than buried in a tenure number you rarely look at.

## KB:repo-005

**Translating an RBI move into your wallet**:

* 25 bp repo cut on a ₹50 L home loan at 8.5% with 20 years left → roughly ₹750/month EMI saving (or ~12 months tenure cut if you keep EMI constant).
* 50 bp repo hike on the same loan → roughly ₹1,500/month higher (or ~24 extra months on tenure).

These are rough shortcuts; the exact number depends on the spread, the reset date, and whether your loan is on monthly-rest or quarterly-rest accounting.
