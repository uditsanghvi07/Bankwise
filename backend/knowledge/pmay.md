# PMAY (high level, educational)

## KB:pmay-001

**PMAY (Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana)** is the umbrella name for government-linked housing affordability initiatives in India. It has gone through multiple phases ("Urban", "Gramin", "Urban 2.0"); eligibility rules, subsidy mechanics, and caps are revised by circulars. Always read the latest official scheme page and confirm the version active on the date your loan is sanctioned.

## KB:pmay-002

**How the subsidy flows** (where applicable):

* You apply for the home loan with a participating lender.
* The lender forwards your application to the Central Nodal Agency (CNA).
* On approval, the subsidy lands as a **lump-sum credit** on your loan principal — your future EMIs drop because the outstanding principal shrinks.
* Subsidies are typically processed *after* documentation, valuation, and technical verification by the lender.

## KB:pmay-003

**Income categories used historically by PMAY-Urban**:

* EWS (Economically Weaker Section): annual household income ≤ ₹3 L.
* LIG (Lower Income Group): ₹3–6 L.
* MIG-I: ₹6–12 L.
* MIG-II: ₹12–18 L.

These slabs were used for differential interest subsidy. The exact ceilings, subsidy rates, and carpet-area limits change between scheme versions — never quote a number from memory in a real loan negotiation.

## KB:pmay-004

**Common reasons applications get rejected**:

* The applicant or co-applicant already owns a pucca house anywhere in India (PMAY is for first-time buyers).
* Carpet area exceeds the prescribed limit for the income slab.
* Property is not located in an eligible city / town listed under the scheme.
* Documents (Aadhaar / PAN / income proof) don't match exactly across bank, employer, and the application form.

Timelines vary widely — some metros process in 2–4 months, others run 6–9 months due to verification queues.
