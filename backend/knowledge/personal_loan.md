# Personal loans (educational)

## KB:personal_loan-001

A **personal loan** is an **unsecured** retail loan with no collateral. Tenures are short (12–60 months, occasionally up to 84), ticket sizes are small to medium (₹50K–₹40 L), and rates are materially higher than secured loans because the bank prices in default risk.

## KB:personal_loan-002

**Indicative interest rate ranges** (as observed in the Indian retail market — verify with the lender):

* Public sector banks: typically 10.5–14% p.a.
* Private banks for salaried prime customers: 10.99–15% p.a.
* NBFCs: 13–24% p.a., higher for thin-file customers.
* Fintech BNPL / instant loans: 18–32% p.a., often with hefty processing and convenience fees.

Always look at the **APR** (effective annual rate after processing fees, GST, and insurance bundling), not just the headline rate.

## KB:personal_loan-003

**When a personal loan makes sense**:

* Genuine medical emergency where insurance reimbursement will follow but cash is needed today.
* Consolidating two or three high-rate credit-card balances into one cheaper EMI (only if the new APR is materially lower *and* you destroy the cards or freeze them).
* Funding a clearly time-bound gap such as a sibling's wedding when income is stable.

**When it does not** — funding lifestyle (vacation, gadget upgrades), gambling, equity/crypto trading, or "investing" in any high-yield scheme. Borrowing at 14% to invest at an *expected* 11% is a guaranteed loss.

## KB:personal_loan-004

**Hidden costs** that catch first-time borrowers:

* Processing fee — 1–3% of the principal, deducted up-front, so you receive *less* than the loan amount but pay EMI on the full sanctioned figure.
* Insurance bundling — some lenders make a credit-life policy quasi-mandatory; you can usually opt out.
* Foreclosure / pre-closure penalty — typically 2–5% of the outstanding principal. RBI bans this on **floating-rate** personal loans for individual borrowers; many personal loans are fixed-rate, so the ban often does not apply. Read the fine print.
* GST on processing fees, foreclosure charges, and insurance — quietly adds another 18%.

## KB:personal_loan-005

**Personal loan vs gold loan** for a short-term emergency:

* Gold loan rates (8–12%) are markedly cheaper than personal loans (12–24%) because they are secured.
* Tenures are shorter (3–24 months) and LTV is capped at 75%.
* Disbursal is faster (sometimes same day) and there is **no** CIBIL pull for small ticket sizes at most NBFCs.

If you have eligible gold lying idle, a gold loan often dominates a personal loan on every dimension except sentiment.
