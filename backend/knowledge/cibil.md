# CIBIL / credit score (educational)

## KB:cibil-001

A **credit score** (CIBIL, Experian, Equifax, CRIF Highmark in India) is a 300–900 summary of repayment behaviour. It is **not** the only input banks use, but it strongly influences eligibility, pricing, and even pre-approved offers. Most retail lenders treat 750+ as the "comfortable" floor and 800+ as elite.

## KB:cibil-002

**Directional factors that usually help over time** (no bureau publishes the exact weights, but the broad pattern is well documented):

* Paying every EMI and credit-card bill on or before the due date — single biggest contributor.
* Keeping revolving credit utilisation under **30%** of total card limits.
* Avoiding more than 2–3 hard enquiries within any 6-month window for unsecured loans.
* Maintaining a **healthy mix** of secured (home, auto, gold loan) and unsecured (card, personal) credit.
* Letting older accounts age — average account age is a positive signal.

## KB:cibil-003

**Common red flags** that pull scores down quickly:

* A single 30+ day payment delay can cost 50–80 points and stay on the report for ~7 years.
* Settling a loan ("settlement") instead of paying it in full leaves a permanent flag worse than a default.
* Maxing out credit-card limits month after month even if you pay in full.
* "Loan stacking" — taking many small unsecured loans within weeks of each other.

## KB:cibil-004

**How long does it take to repair?** Recovery is gradual and depends on the size of the negative event:

* Two clean billing cycles (60 days) often add 20–40 points after a small slip.
* Fully closing a high-utilisation card and waiting 90 days can add 30–60 points.
* A "settled" or "written-off" tag rarely clears in under 12–24 months even with perfect new behaviour.

## KB:cibil-005

BankWise AI does **not** replicate the proprietary bureau scoring model. Any in-app "simulator" is a **directional, illustrative** tool for educational decisions only — for the real number, pull your free annual CIBIL report directly from the bureau and dispute inaccuracies in writing.
