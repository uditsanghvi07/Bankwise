# Income tax and 80C (educational, India)

## KB:tax-001

India runs **two parallel personal income-tax regimes**: the older "old regime" with ~70 deductions and exemptions, and the newer "new regime" with broader, lower slabs but almost no deductions. From AY 2024-25 the new regime is the **default** for individuals — explicitly opt for the old regime in the ITR if you want to keep claiming deductions.

## KB:tax-002

**Section 80C — the most-used deduction (old regime only)**: combined cap of **₹1.5 L/year** across:

* EPF contributions (statutory, already deducted from salary).
* PPF contributions.
* ELSS mutual funds (3-year lock-in, equity exposure).
* Life-insurance premiums (within rules on cover/premium ratio).
* Home-loan principal repayment.
* Tuition fees for up to 2 children (school portion only).
* NSC, 5-year tax-saver FD, Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana.

You cannot stack the same rupee in two of these — the cap is on total contributions across the basket, not per item.

## KB:tax-003

**Other commonly used old-regime sections**:

* 80D — health insurance premium (₹25K self/family + ₹50K parents if senior).
* 80CCD(1B) — extra ₹50K for NPS Tier-I (over and above 80C).
* 80E — interest on education loan, no upper limit, max 8 years.
* 80EEA — additional home-loan interest deduction for first-time buyers (eligibility window updated by Budget; verify date).
* 24(b) — home-loan interest up to ₹2 L for self-occupied (separate from 80C).
* HRA — house rent allowance for salaried tenants (requires rent receipts and landlord PAN above ₹1 L/year rent).

## KB:tax-004

**When the new regime usually wins**: salaried with no home loan, no rent (HRA), and 80C contributions below ~₹1 L. The lower slab structure plus the standard deduction (₹75K from FY 24-25) often beats the old regime simply on math. **When the old regime usually wins**: salaried with a self-occupied home loan, HRA in a metro, full 80C + 80D + NPS-1B utilised.

Run the actual numbers in the tax calculator on the income-tax e-filing portal before locking the regime — there is no universal rule.

## KB:tax-005

**Tax on investments** (commonly missed):

* Equity LTCG (held > 12 months): 12.5% on gains over ₹1.25 L/year, post-Jul-2024 rules.
* Equity STCG (held ≤ 12 months): 20%.
* Debt mutual funds bought after 1-Apr-2023: gains taxed at slab rate, no indexation.
* FD interest: fully taxable at slab; banks deduct 10% TDS once interest crosses ₹40K/year (₹50K for seniors).
* PPF / EPF / Sukanya / sovereign-gold-bond redemption: fully tax-free under current rules.
